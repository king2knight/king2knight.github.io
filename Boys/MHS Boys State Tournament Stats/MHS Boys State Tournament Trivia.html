MHS Boys State Tournament Trivia

 

Since 1938, McPherson High School has made it to the state tournament 47 times or an equivalent to an average appearance greater than every other year.  Frequent appearances at the state tournament over the years have created a community following unrivaled by any other school.  With the overwhelming support of the fans, the Bullpups have played well at the state tournament.  Their resume includes 13 state championships, 26 championship title games, and an overall winning percentage of 67.5%.

 

Despite these successes, it could be argued that more state championships would have been won had the classification system always been as we know it today.  For example, on three occasions, 1961, 1965, and 1967, state championship caliber teams were defeated in the state tournament by Wyandotte High School.  This huge Kansas City school had a virtual dynasty on state championships (10 in 14 years from 1956-69).  Wyandotte may have been the only team capable of defeating McPherson those three seasons.  In today’s classification system, McPherson would have not been a large enough school to play Wyandotte during their dynasty years.
