MHS Boys State Tournament "What If's"

 

Prior to 1969, McPherson was classified as a AA school. The AA classification included schools with the largest enrollments, followed in descending order by Class A, B, and BB. Unlike today's expanded classification system, having just four classifications meant that within a given classification there was a wide disparity in enrollment. In McPherson's case, much larger metropolitan teams from Wichita, Kansas City, and Topeka were also in Class AA. When it came time to qualify for and compete for state championships, McPherson was often in a "David vs. Goliath" situation. Despite this, McPherson was more than competitive. However, this large enrollment disparity (Wyandotte 2,405 enrollment vs. 585 for MHS in 1965) cost us potential state championships that may have been possible in the expanded classification system currently used.

 

The table below lists pre-1969 MHS losses to much larger schools either in a Regional (Sub-State) or State games to AA teams, that by today's classification system we would not have competed against. What if McPherson had competed against schools its own size in Regional (Sub-State) and State play prior to 1969? How many state championships would have been won in those seasons where good McPherson teams were defeated by larger schools outside of our current classification?
