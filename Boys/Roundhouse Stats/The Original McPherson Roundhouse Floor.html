The Original McPherson Roundhouse Floor

1963-2007 (Men’s Games)

 

The original floor at the McPherson Roundhouse was replaced after the 2006-07 season. Looking back on all the men's games played on that original floor, some interesting statistics were derived. Listed below are some of the more compelling facts that are a testimony to the term "home court advantage".

 

If These Boards Could Talk, What Would They Say?

 

We remember winning 453 games and only losing 83 games in 44 seasons (84.5%)

 

We remember winning 239 league games and only losing 44 (84.5%)

 

We remember winning 71 non-league games and only losing 6 (92.2%)

 

We remember winning 99 invitational games and only losing 30 (76.7%)

 

We remember winning 43 sub-state games and only losing 3 (93.5%)

 

We remember the longest winning streak of 66 games (1/23/92 to 1/24/97)

 

We remember only 1 loss between 12/16/88 and 1/24/97 (preventing a 105 game home court winning streak)

 

We remember an average winning margin of 18 points per game over 44 seasons

 

We remember 5 games where we scored more than 100 points per game

 

We remember the most points scored in a game was 106, and that it happened twice

 

We remember the largest margin of victory was 55 points, and that it happened three times

 

We also remember 6 other games where the margin of victory was greater than 50 points
