History and Tradition

 

Since 1937, McPherson High School has been playing basketball as a member of either the Central Kansas League (CKL) or more recently the Ark Valley League (AVL) and Ark Valley-Chisholm Trail League (AVCTL). During the CKL years, the league name stayed the same, but the league alignment changed eight times. The MHS League Affiliations Since 1937 table illustrates the various league changes and their respective durations.

 

While the league alignment was changing over the years so was the game of basketball itself. What started out as a rather slow deliberate game has slowly evolved into the up-tempo game we know today. As the rules and coaching philosophies changed, the game became faster resulting in increased scoring. This can be seen from the bar graph entitled Seasonal Scoring Average by MHS Teams Since 1938. This graph illustrates changes in seasonal scoring averages from 1938 to the present. In the 1930's and during most of the 1940's, the seasonal scoring average by MHS teams was less than 40 points per game. In the 1950's, the scoring average climbed up into the 50 point per game range. From the 1960's on, the seasonal scoring averages have generally been in the 60 point per game range with a few seasons averaging more than 70 points per game.

 

As league alignments and the game were gradually changing, there remained one constant, a winning tradition. This has been graphically illustrated in the bar chart entitled Summary of McPherson's Basketball Seasons 1938-2022.

 

 

Since 1938, McPherson has enjoyed uncommon success on the basketball floor as evidenced by an overall winning percentage of 76.0%. What sustains such tradition year after year?

 

Coaching:

With the exception of Harold Johnston's 1 year stint, McPherson has had stability at the position of head coach. Jack Randle coached for 19 seasons, Jay Frazier for 26 seasons, Mike Henson for 12 seasons, and Kurt Kinnamon for 27 seasons and counting. All of these coaches stressed teamwork and execution which enabled MHS to beat more athletic teams regularly.

 

Tradition:

Tradition sets the expectations high which is a great motivator. Young players look up to their high school heroes and aspire to be just like them someday.

 

Teamwork:

Teamwork encompasses not only the way the players execute on the floor, but the way the school, the parents, and community at large support the program. The tradition is alive and well thanks to the dedication of the coaches, players, parents, fans, and the community. Perhaps the winning tradition started back with the success of the Globe Refiners in the mid 1930's. Whatever the origin, McPherson has long been known as a basketball town.
