History and Tradition

 

Since 1969-70 season, McPherson High School has been playing girls basketball first as an independent, and then in 1978 as member of the Ark Valley League (AVL) and later the Ark Valley-Chisholm Trail League (AVCTL). During the AVCTL years, the league name stayed the same, but the league alignment changed 8 times.

 

While the league alignment was changing over the years so was the game of basketball itself. What started out as a rather slow deliberate game has slowly evolved into a more up-tempo game. As the rules, coaching philosophies, and the players' skill sets improved, the game became faster which resulted in increased scoring. This can be seen from the bar graph entitled Seasonal Scoring Average by MHS Teams Since 1970. This graph illustrates changes in seasonal scoring averages from 1970 to the present. In the 1970's, the per game scoring average by MHS teams was in the 40's. Starting the 1980's and moving forward, the per game scoring average climbed up into the 50+ points per game range. In certain years, the per game scoring average increased into the 60's.

 

As league alignments, and the game itself, were gradually changing, there remained one constant, a winning tradition. This has been graphically illustrated in the bar chart entitled Summary of McPherson's Basketball Seasons 1969-2024.

 

 

Since 1970, McPherson has enjoyed uncommon success on the basketball floor as evidenced by an overall winning percentage of 77.1%. What sustains such tradition year after year?

 

Coaching:

For the first 20 years, McPherson had relatively short tenures at the position of head coach. Ann Killingsworth coached for 5 seasons, Mike Fitzwater for 2 seasons, John Hoffman for 7 seasons, Brian Cordel for 3 seasons, and Nancy Loescher for 6 seasons. Starting in 1992, the coaching position has been stable. Scott Schaefer coached for 13 seasons, and his assistant, Chris Strathman has been at the helm for 19 years and counting. All of these coaches stressed teamwork and execution which enabled MHS to regularly beat more athletic teams.

 

Tradition:

Tradition sets the expectations high at MHS which is a great motivator. Young players look up to their high school heroes and aspire to be just like them someday.

 

Teamwork:

Teamwork encompasses not only the way the players execute on the floor, but the way the school, the parents, and community at large support the program. The tradition is alive and well thanks to the dedication of the coaches, players, parents, fans, and the community. Perhaps the boys' winning tradition rubbed off on the girls' program. Whatever the reason, McPherson has been rightly known a basketball town.
